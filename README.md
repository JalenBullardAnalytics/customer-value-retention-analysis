# Customer Value & Retention Analysis Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project analyzes Customer Lifetime Value (CLV), retention performance, customer inactivity, and growth opportunities using Excel, SQL (Google BigQuery), and Tableau.

The objective was to identify high-value customers, understand retention risks, evaluate industry and regional performance, and provide actionable business recommendations through data-driven insights.

## Business Questions

### 1. Who are our most valuable customers?

* Identify the top 5 customers by Customer Lifetime Value (CLV).
* Determine which customer segments contribute the most value.

### 2. Which industries generate the highest value?

* Analyze CLV across industries.
* Identify the highest-performing regions within each industry.

### 3. Where are we losing customers?

* Analyze customer inactivity across customer segments, industries, and regions.
* Evaluate retention performance across customer groups.

### 4. Where are the greatest growth opportunities?

* Identify industries, segments, and regions that present the strongest opportunities for future customer value growth.

## Key Findings

### Value Insight

Enterprise customers contribute approximately 43% of total Customer Lifetime Value, making them the most valuable customer segment.

### Retention Risk

Small Business customers account for the highest number of inactive customers (363), representing the largest retention risk.

### Industry Opportunity

Real Estate generates the highest Customer Lifetime Value at approximately $14.5M, making it the strongest industry growth opportunity.

### Regional Insight

The West region generates the highest overall Customer Lifetime Value, with California, Nevada, and Washington serving as the primary contributors.

---

## Business Recommendations

### Retain High-Value Customers

Prioritize retention initiatives for Enterprise customers due to their significant contribution to overall customer value.

### Address Retention Risks

Develop targeted retention strategies for Small Business customers to reduce inactivity and improve long-term customer value.

### Expand High-Performing Industries

Increase focus on Real Estate and other top-performing industries to maximize customer value growth.

### Leverage Regional Strengths

Continue investing in high-performing West region markets while evaluating opportunities to improve performance in lower-performing regions.
